The Thing You Are Doing

From Ask MetaFilter, user stoneweaver, in response to the question “How [can I] get that military focus, time & project management skills, excellent organization and self-discipline without the actual military experience?”:

You are absolutely going to hate my answer, which I’m sorry for. The answer is: Just do it. When the alarm goes off and you don’t want to get up? Just get up and do it. Tell yourself “Just for today, I’m going to just get up.” When the project needs to be done, just do it. Say to yourself, “This is going to take a half hour, and I can do it for the next half hour.”

Being organized and having time management aren’t easy. There’s not some magical thing the people in the military know that you don’t know. It’s not any more fun or whatever for them. It’s just that they aren’t expecting it to be. It doesn’t keep them from doing it.

Stop thinking of it as a choice and recognize that it’s just the thing you are doing. When the alarm goes off, you get up. Full stop. When you are sitting down to finish paperwork, that’s what you’re doing. Full stop. When you have other fun things to do, things you would rather do, recognize that they’re not the priority and you have to do first things first. Full stop.

It’s about not giving yourself a break. But do start small. Instead of tackling it all Right Now, decide that for the next month you’re going to get out of bed when the alarm goes off no exceptions. Once you’ve got that, move on to the next thing. One at a time.
